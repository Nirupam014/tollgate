Counts, not proof The coverage, gate, and findings numbers below are raw counts observed in the wild. A random public repo has no ground truth, so none of them are “validated correct.”

Methodology & what these numbers do not say

Static, no execution. Tollgate AST-parses files; it never imports or runs target code. The runner clones shallowly, disables credential prompts, and runs no build/test hooks.
Counts vs. correctness. Coverage, gate split, and the findings histogram are descriptive statistics over public repos. They describe what the scanner did, not whether each verdict is right. The only correctness claim is the precision figure above, and it is exactly as strong as the number of findings hand-adjudicated.
Responsible framing. Findings are shown anonymized by default. Naming a repo is a public claim about someone’s project; with a non-zero false-positive rate, a responsible-disclosure tone is preferred over a “look at these bad repos” one.
Reproducibility. The repo population and every analyzed repo’s commit SHA are pinned, so anyone can regenerate these numbers against identical trees.
Descriptive statistics are counts measured across public repositories, not validated-correct figures. Precision (when shown) is the sole adjudicated correctness claim and carries a Wilson 95% confidence interval. Re-running on the pinned repo list and SHAs reproduces the report.
